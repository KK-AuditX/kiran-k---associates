## What Is Information System Audit (ISA) and Why Every Indian Business Needs One in 2026
An Information System Audit evaluates your IT infrastructure, data security, and digital controls. With ICAI's new ISAS framework rolling out in 2026, here's what every Indian business owner needs to know.
Every business today runs on technology — ERP systems, cloud storage, payroll software, email servers, customer databases. But here's a question most business owners don't ask often enough: Is someone actually checking if all of that is secure, accurate, and compliant?
That's exactly what an Information System Audit (ISA) does.

## What Is an Information System Audit?
An Information System Audit is a structured review of your organisation's IT infrastructure, digital processes, data management practices, and cybersecurity controls. Think of it as a financial audit — but instead of reviewing your books, an IS Auditor reviews your systems.
It checks:
•	Access controls — Who has access to what data, and should they?
•	Data integrity — Is the data in your systems accurate and tamper-proof?
•	Security compliance — Are your systems protected against cyber threats?
•	Backup and recovery — Can your business recover quickly if something goes wrong?
•	IT governance — Are your technology decisions aligned with your business goals and regulatory requirements?

## Why Does It Matter Right Now?
India's digital economy is growing at an extraordinary pace. With this growth comes a sharp rise in cyber incidents. CERT-In recorded over 1.9 million cybersecurity incidents in India in 2023, and the number continues to rise year on year.
More importantly, regulators are catching up. The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) mandates annual IS Audits for banks and NBFCs. SEBI requires IS Audits for all registered market intermediaries under its Cybersecurity and Cyber Resilience Framework (CSCRF). And with India's Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023 now in force, businesses that handle personal data are increasingly expected to demonstrate audit readiness.
The biggest development of 2026 is from ICAI itself. The Institute of Chartered Accountants of India — through its Digital Accounting and Assurance Board (DAAB) — has proposed a first-of-its-kind Information Systems Audit Standards (ISAS) framework, covering 11 specific standards. Once notified, IS Audits in India will shift from a best-practice activity to a formal compliance obligation.

## Who Should Get an IS Audit?
Many business owners assume IS Audits are only for large companies or banks. That's not the case. You need an IS Audit if:
•	Your business uses cloud-based accounting, HR, or CRM systems
•	You store customer data — names, phone numbers, financial details
•	You are an NBFC, fintech, stockbroker, or insurance company
•	You are a startup preparing for due diligence or funding rounds
•	You have remote teams accessing company systems
•	You want to achieve ISO 27001 certification

## What Does an IS Audit Look Like in Practice?
A qualified IS Auditor — typically a Chartered Accountant with an ISA 3.0 certification from ICAI — will go through a structured process:
1.	Planning — Understanding your IT environment, business processes, and regulatory requirements
2.	Risk Assessment — Identifying high-risk areas such as privileged access, third-party vendors, and data storage
3.	Evidence Collection — Reviewing system logs, access rights, configurations, and backup records
4.	Testing — Checking whether controls are working as intended
5.	Reporting — A clear, structured report with findings, risk ratings, and recommendations

## The Bottom Line
A business that hasn't had an IS Audit is essentially flying blind on its technology risks. Financial risks get attention because they show up on balance sheets. Technology risks are often invisible — until a breach, a data leak, or a regulatory notice lands on your desk.
With ICAI's ISAS framework on the horizon and regulators like RBI, SEBI, and CERT-In tightening their expectations, an IS Audit is no longer optional. It is the foundation of responsible business governance in a digital age.
